Best Institute for ITI Courses

Industrial Training Institute (ITI) courses provide hands-on vocational training for students seeking practical skills and immediate employment opportunities in various technical fields. Our ITI programs are designed to equip students with industry-relevant skills and knowledge.

Why Choose Our ITI Courses?

At Wintech Institute of Management & Technology, we offer comprehensive ITI programs that blend theoretical knowledge with practical training. Our courses are designed to meet industry standards and prepare students for successful careers in technical fields. With state-of-the-art workshops, experienced instructors, and industry partnerships, we provide the best training environment for aspiring technicians and tradespeople.

All our ITI courses have a duration of two years (four semesters) and are open to students who have completed 10th standard. We provide placement assistance and practical exposure to ensure our graduates are job-ready from day one.

Our ITI Course Programs

ITI in Electrician

Comprehensive training in electrical systems, wiring, installation, troubleshooting and maintenance

An ITI in Electrician is a vocational training course that helps students develop foundational skills required to work with electrical systems, wiring, installation, troubleshooting and maintenance of electrical equipment or appliances. Being comprehensive technical training, that provides students with the opportunity to pursue careers in the electrical industry.

Eligibility

After 10th

Duration

Two Years (Four Semesters)

Course Subjects

  • Electrical Engineering Basics
  • Electrical Instruments & Measurements
  • Electrical Wiring & Installation
  • DC & AC Machines
  • Basic Electronics
  • Transformers
  • Power Generation, Transmission & Distribution
  • Electrical Maintenance & Safety Rules

ITI in Pipefitter

Training in pipe installation, repair, and maintenance for industrial and construction applications

Industrial Training Institute (ITI) in Pipe Fitter teaches students how to install, repair, and maintain pipes and piping systems in industrial plants, buildings, and water distribution and treatment system. Students will learn to cut, weld, and fit pipes, and this is a great course to choose for those who want to work in a hands-on career in the plumbing and construction industries.

Eligibility

After 10th

Duration

Two Years (Four Semesters)

Semester I & II

  • Introduction to Pipe Fitting
  • Safety Rules & PPE
  • Types of Pipes (MS, SS, PVC)
  • Tools & Measuring Instruments
  • Basic Marking & Layout
  • Types of Pipe Joints - Threaded, Flanged, Welded
  • Basics of Welding & Gas Cutting
  • Pipe Fittings - Elbows, Tees, Reducers

Semester III & IV

  • Advanced Pipe Layout & Fabrication
  • Pipe Supports & Expansion Joints
  • Hydraulic Testing & Pressure Calculation
  • Maintenance Procedures
  • Preventive Maintenance of Pipe Systems
  • Fault Finding & Troubleshooting
  • Modern Piping Systems (Oil & Gas, Power Plants)
  • Industrial Safety & First Aid

ITI in Mechanic

Comprehensive training in vehicle and machinery repair, maintenance, and diagnostics

The ITI course in Mechanic is a vocational course that will allow students to learn how to fix, service, and maintain machinery, vehicles, and equipment. Students will engage in engine overhauling, diagnostics of vehicle systems, and troubleshooting of mechanical systems. This vocational course is suited to people who enjoy hands-on jobs involving vehicles and machinery.

Eligibility

After 10th

Duration

Two Years (Four Semesters)

Semester I & II

  • Introduction to Automobile Industry
  • Safety rules, PPE, tools & equipment
  • Basics of Internal Combustion (IC) Engines
  • Engine Components & Working Principles
  • Fuel Supply System (Carburetor, Fuel Injection)
  • Cooling & Lubrication Systems
  • Transmission Systems (Clutch, Gearbox, Differential)

ITI in Machinist

Training in machine operation, maintenance, and precision machining techniques

ITI Machinist teaches students to operate and maintain machines used in a factory or manufacturing process. Examples include machine tools, cutting metals, and precision work. After graduating, students will get jobs in factories, workshops, or start their own business. Choose the best institutes of ITI courses, with hands-on training and the latest technologies and machinery to improve their skills and employability.

Eligibility

After 10th

Duration

Two Years (Four Semesters)

Semester I & II

  • Introduction to Machining
  • Workshop Safety & PPE
  • Types of Materials (Metals, Alloys)
  • Tools & Measuring Instruments
  • Lathe Operations & Tools
  • Drilling, Boring & Grinding Machines
  • Material Properties & Machining Principles

Semester III & IV

  • Advanced Machining - Milling, Shaping, Slotting
  • Thread Cutting, Gear Cutting
  • Maintenance of Machine Tools
  • Modern Machining Techniques
  • CNC Basics (if included)
  • Industrial Safety & Maintenance
  • Workshop Management

ITI in Plumber

Training in plumbing systems, pipe fitting, drainage, and water supply installation

The ITI in Plumber program educates students about the installation, maintenance, and repair of plumbing systems, including pipe fitting, drainage, and water supply. ITI in Plumber job opportunities exist in the construction and maintenance fields, or plumbing students can start their own plumbing business.

Eligibility

After 10th

Duration

Two Years (Four Semesters)

Course Subjects

  • Introduction to Plumbing
  • Tools & Equipment (Spanners, Wrenches, Cutters)
  • Safety & PPE
  • Types of Pipes (PVC, GI, Copper)
  • Pipe Fittings & Joints
  • Basic Water Supply Systems
  • Drainage Systems (Sewage, Waste Water)
  • Installation of Sanitary Fixtures
  • Hot & Cold Water Systems
  • Pumps & Valves
  • Leak Detection & Maintenance
  • Basic Estimation & Costing

ITI in Drafter

Training in technical drawing, blueprints, and CAD for construction and manufacturing

ITI in Drafter helps students develop drawings and blueprints in construction through drafting methods and CAD. This program is suited for the design and planning career paths. Graduates can pursue careers as drafters, CAD operators, or independent workers.

Eligibility

After 10th

Duration

Two Years (Four Semesters)

Semester I & II

  • Introduction to Drafting
  • Basics of Drawing Tools
  • Geometrical Constructions
  • Scales & Dimensioning
  • Drafting Standards & Conventions
  • Orthographic Projection
  • Sectional Views
  • Isometric Projection
  • Introduction to CAD software

Semester III & IV

  • Advanced CAD techniques
  • Mechanical/Civil Drafting standards
  • Drawing of machine parts, structural components
  • Modern Drafting Techniques
  • 3D Modeling basics
  • Blueprint reading & interpretation
  • Project planning & drawing standards
